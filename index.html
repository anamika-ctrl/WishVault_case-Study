01 · Problem Space

The chaos of modern shopping

Indian shoppers juggle 6–8 different apps for different categories. But none of them talk to each other — so keeping track of what you want is entirely on you.

🗂️
Problem 01

Fragmented Wishlists

Saved items are scattered across Amazon, Myntra, Nykaa, Meesho, and more. There is no single view of everything you want.

📉
Problem 02

Missed Price Drops

Without a centralized tracker, users miss discounts and flash sales — often discovering a deal only after it's gone.

🔗
Problem 03

No Cross-Platform Sharing

Sharing a wishlist means copying individual links from different apps. There is no single, shareable list to send.

⏱️
Problem 04

High Friction to Save & Revisit

Users resort to screenshots, notes, and browser bookmarks just to remember products — making rediscovery tedious and unreliable.

02 · User Research

5 conversations. 5 different hacks.

I ran informal interviews with 5 active online shoppers to understand how they currently manage products they want to buy — and what breaks down.

Interview Questions

Five questions designed to surface real behaviour — not hypotheticals.

Q1 How do you keep track of things you want to buy?
Q2 Which shopping apps do you use the most?
Q3 Have you ever forgotten a product you wanted to buy?
Q4 Have you ever missed a sale or price drop?
Q5 If one app could save products from every shopping website, would you use it?

Findings

User Current Method Key Pain
User 1 Amazon wishlist + Screenshots Forgot products saved elsewhere
User 2 WhatsApp links Difficult to organise
User 3 Notes app No price change alerts
User 4 Browser bookmarks Hard to revisit later
User 5 Separate wishlists per app Wishes for one unified place

All 5 users had developed their own personal workaround — none of which actually solved the core problem.

04 · Competitive Analysis

The gap no one is filling

Existing tools solve fragments of the problem — coupon finders, price trackers, creator storefronts. None of them unify cross-platform wishlists, price alerts, and sharing in a single India-first app.

Competitor Strength Gap / Limitation
Honey Coupon codes & rewards No unified wishlist; limited relevance in Indian marketplaces
CamelCamelCamel Amazon price history Amazon-only; no support for Flipkart, Myntra, Nykaa, Meesho, or Ajio
PriceSpy Multi-retailer comparison Limited India coverage; focuses on price comparison, not wishlist management
Smartprix Indian store comparison Electronics-only; no cross-category personal wishlist or collaborative features
Wishlink Creator storefronts Built for influencers — not personal wishlists, price tracking, or everyday shoppers

The Indian shopping ecosystem is growing rapidly, but users still manage wishlists separately within each platform — creating a clear opportunity for a unified shopping companion.
